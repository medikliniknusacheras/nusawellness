Explanations:

Red Blood Cells (RBC): Measures the number of red blood cells in your blood, which carry oxygen.

Haemoglobin (Hb): The protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen.

Red Cell Indices: Includes MCH, MCV, MCHC, and PCV to evaluate the size and hemoglobin content of red blood cells.

Platelet Count: Measures the number of platelets, which help with blood clotting.

White Blood Cells (WBC): Measures the number of white blood cells, which fight infection.

Differential Count (DC): Breaks down the different types of white blood cells.

Peripheral Blood Film (PBF): Examines the physical characteristics of blood cells under a microscope.

Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate (ESR): Measures how quickly red blood cells settle at the bottom of a test tube; can indicate inflammation.

Urea, Sodium, Potassium, Chloride, Creatinine: Tests for kidney function and electrolyte balance.

Estimated - GFR (CKD-EPI): Estimates kidney filtration rate.

Calcium, Corrected Calcium, Phosphate: Tests for bone health and metabolism.

Uric Acid: Tests for gout.

Glucose: Screens for diabetes.

Total Protein, Albumin, Globulin: Measures proteins in the blood to assess liver function and overall health.

Albumin/Globulin Ratio: The ratio of albumin to globulin proteins in the blood.

Total Bilirubin: Measures bilirubin levels, which can indicate liver or bile duct problems.

Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP): An enzyme related to the bile ducts; often increased when they are blocked.

Alanine Transaminase (ALT/SGPT), Aspartate Transaminase (AST/SGOT): Enzymes that help diagnose liver damage.

Gamma-Glutamyl Transferase (GGT): Enzyme that helps detect liver and bile duct injury.

Fibrosis 4 Index (FIB4): A non-invasive scoring system to estimate the amount of liver scarring.

Total Cholesterol, HDL-Cholesterol, LDL-Cholesterol, Triglycerides: Measures lipids in the blood to assess heart disease risk.

Total Chol/HDL Ratio: The ratio of total cholesterol to HDL cholesterol, an indicator of heart disease risk.

Urine FEME: A comprehensive urine test for detecting urinary tract infections and other abnormalities.

Free T3 (FT3), Free T4 (FT4), Thyroid Stimulating Hormone (TSH): Tests for thyroid function.

Insulin Growth Factor 1 (IGF-1): A hormone important for growth and development, related to pituitary function.

Cortisol (AM/PM): Measures adrenal gland function and stress response.

DHEA-Sulphate: A hormone produced by the adrenal glands, important for assessing adrenal function.

Estradiol, Progesterone, Testosterone: Hormones important for reproductive health.

Sex Hormone Binding Globulin (SHBG): A protein that binds to sex hormones, such as testosterone and estradiol.

Serum Insulin: Measures the amount of insulin in the blood, important for diabetes diagnosis and management.

Growth Hormone: Measures the amount of growth hormone in the blood, important for growth and metabolism.

Vitamin D: Measures the level of Vitamin D in the blood, important for bone health and immune function.

Luteinizing Hormone (LH): Helps regulate the function of the ovaries and testes.

Follicle Stimulating Hormone (FSH): Important for the growth and maturation of eggs in women and sperm production in men.

Prolactin: A hormone that stimulates milk production after childbirth.

CEA (Carcinoembryonic Antigen): A marker that can indicate certain types of cancer.

AFP (Alpha-fetoprotein): A marker used to detect liver cancer and other diseases.

CA 125: A marker often used to detect ovarian cancer.

CA 15-3: A marker used to monitor breast cancer.

CA 19-9: A marker used primarily to monitor pancreatic cancer.

Hs-CRP (High-sensitivity C-Reactive Protein): A marker for inflammation in the body.

Homocysteine: An amino acid linked to higher risk of coronary artery disease.
